Highlighted Research Experiences

  • Design a sharding protocol integrated with multiparty virtual payment channels: Designed cross-shard transaction protocols using payment channels and threshold signatures. We designed a sharding protocol integrated with multiparty virtual payment channels (MPCs) to enable secure and decentralized cross-shard transactions without relying on single-point intermediaries. The protocol leverages a dynamically formed MPC group to maximize channel capacity and supports transactions involving multiple senders and receivers, thereby greatly improving blockchain transaction throughput.
  • Designed an innovative distributed sharding protocol for blockchains: I proposed a novel sharding protocol that leverages payment channels to enable efficient cross-shard transactions, enhancing blockchain scalability and enabling distributed storage. The protocol incorporates a threshold signature scheme to batch-sign messages for higher throughput and introduces an atomicity mechanism that ensures cross-shard consistency while achieving constant communication complexity O(1). Compared to state-of-the-art protocols, it reduces latency by over 40%, offering a significant improvement in performance.
  • Designed an optimal virtual payment channel (VPC) construction protocol: We designed a new metric to evaluate both the number of intermediaries and the capacity of constructed virtual payment channels (VPCs). Building on a recursive VPC construction protocol starting from the minimal one-intermediary, two-party architecture, I optimized VPC construction for multiple user pairs using a game-theoretic algorithm.
  • Designed an innovative rebalancing protocol for payment channel networks: We proposed a distributed balance-aware routing protocol that achieves high throughput while accounting for channel balances. By modifying the original HTLC to improve efficiency and adapt it to balance-aware routing, the protocol guarantees atomicity of transactions. Evaluations using real banking transaction history demonstrate a nearly 100% success ratio and success volume, highlighting its effectiveness.
