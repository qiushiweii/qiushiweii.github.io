HIGHLIGHTED RESEARCH PROJECTS

  • Designed an optimal virtual payment channel (VPC) construction protocol

    • First author, published at 2024 IEEE INFOCOM

    Traditional payment channel networks (PCNs) rely on intermediaries to route payments between users without direct channels, which limits efficiency and scalability. To overcome this issue, we developed Thor, a recursive VPC construction protocol that introduces the Capacity-to-Number-of-Intermediaries Ratio (CNIR), a metric that considers both channel capacity and collateral usage. By leveraging a game-theoretic optimization algorithm, Thor efficiently constructs VPC networks and significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods in terms of both capacity and CNIR.

  • Designed an innovative rebalancing protocol for payment channel networks (PCNs)

    • First author, published at 2025 IEEE ICC

    PCNs improve blockchain scalability through off-chain transactions but suffer from balance depletion due to unidirectional payments. We proposed a distributed balance-aware routing protocol, BAR, that achieves high throughput while accounting for channel balances. By modifying the original Hashed Timelock Contract (HTLC) to improve efficiency and adapt it to balance-aware routing, BAR guarantees transaction atomicity. We used real banking transaction data to evaluate BAR and demonstrated that it achieves a nearly 100% success ratio and success volume, highlighting its effectiveness.
