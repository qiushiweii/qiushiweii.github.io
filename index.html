Qiushi Wei

Summary

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Colorado School of Mines, expecting to graduate in December 2025. My research focuses on networking, blockchain, payment channel networks, sharding, distributed systems, and cybersecurity. I have published in top venues such as INFOCOM, ICDCS, ICC, and ICMLA, and I am actively seeking a tenure-track faculty position starting in 2026.

Education

  • M.S. in Applied Chemistry, Colorado School of Mines, 2018–2021
  • M.S. in Chemistry and Physics of Polymers, South China University of Technology, 2015–2018
  • B.S. in Chemistry, Hubei University, 2011–2015

Highlighted Projects

  • Blockchain Sharding Protocols: Designed cross-shard transaction protocols using payment channels and threshold signatures.
  • Virtual Payment Channels (VPC): Developed optimization algorithms and recursive VPC construction with game-theory guarantees.
  • Balance-Aware Routing: Invented BAR protocol for efficient routing in payment channel networks with atomicity guarantees.
